Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2025

Introduction

We take your privacy seriously. This policy explains how we use Google Analytics to collect and process information when you visit our website.

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google, Inc., to understand how users interact with our website. Google Analytics collects data such as:

  • The pages you visit on our website
  • The time you spend on our site
  • Your referring website (the site you came from)
  • Your browser type, operating system, and device information
  • Other website interactions such as clicks and scrolling behavior

This data helps us improve the functionality and content of our website. Google Analytics does not collect personally identifiable information such as your name or contact details.

Cookies

Google Analytics uses cookies, which are small text files stored on your device. These cookies allow us to analyze how users interact with our site, but the data is anonymized and does not identify you personally.

You can opt out of Google Analytics by using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on, available here, or by adjusting your browser settings to block cookies.

IP Anonymization

We have enabled IP anonymization in Google Analytics, which means that Google will not store or process your full IP address. Instead, your IP address will be truncated within member states of the European Union or other parties to the Agreement on the European Economic Area.

How to Manage Cookies

You can control the use of cookies on our site through your browser settings. Please refer to the All About Cookies website for more details on how to manage and delete cookies.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our privacy policy periodically. Any changes will be reflected on this page, and we will update the “last modified” date at the top of this page.
